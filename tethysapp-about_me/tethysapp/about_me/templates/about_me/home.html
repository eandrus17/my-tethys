I played volleyball this morning so that's what I've been thinking about so I'll tell you a little bit about my volleyball career.

I started playing my 7th grade year at Jackson Street Middle School. I had never touched a volleyball before. I baaaaaarely made the team, like chosen 11th or 12th out of 12. Luckily I guess our coach some some potential or something. 8th grade, I played. I still remember the first time I ever served the ball over the net overhand (it was on my birthday).
My freshman year, I started as a middle blocker. I had a coach that really pushed me to be better and stronger. She is 6'2" and one of my heroes. My sophomore year, I played varsity ball under some seniors that would threaten to break my fingers occasionally. To keep me humble I'm sure. They're great. Meanwhile, every summer I came to the BYU volleyball camps, and I probably owe making the team to those camps. I played with the likes of Maddie Graham who played on the BYU volleyball team until a year ago when she graduated.
My junior year, I finally started hitting strides as a player and even had an article written about me: Click here to see it. Don't get too excited. It's short and I'm from a small town so it actually was pretty common for the high school sports to be in the paper.
My senior year, I was a captain of the team and we did some pretty cool things, like winning a tournament that our school hadn't won in 20 years, without our head coach. At the end of the season I was named one of the league MVPs. So that was fun. Here's a picture: I changed it to a link because none of pictures are working but that's fine. Good times.
